HackerRank can secure a test. It cannot sit in on a conversation.

That is the whole reason "Trueyy vs HackerRank" is a slightly wrong question. People searching for a HackerRank proctoring alternative are usually mixing two different stages of the same funnel. HackerRank is an assessment platform: it hands a candidate a coding test inside a controlled window and watches that window closely. Trueyy is live interview integrity: it sits beside a Zoom, Google Meet, or Microsoft Teams call and reads device signals while a real conversation happens. Both are about honesty. They just guard different rooms.

This is a fair comparison, not a takedown. HackerRank's proctoring is genuinely good at its job. The mistake teams make is assuming that because the assessment stage is covered, the live interview is too. It usually isn't. Here is the honest line between the two, and where each one belongs.

## What HackerRank proctoring actually does

HackerRank is a skills-assessment platform first. Its proctoring exists to protect the integrity of a test a candidate takes, usually a timed coding challenge, before or instead of a human ever gets on a call. It ships in three escalating tiers:

- **Secure Mode** (on every test): enforces full-screen, blocks copy and paste, prevents multiple monitors, and alerts when a candidate switches tabs. This is environmental — it restricts what the test window allows.
- **Proctor Mode** (an AI add-on for tests created after the July 2025 release): layers on AI screenshot analysis, plagiarism detection, webcam anomaly detection, periodic image snapshots, photo ID, and a session-replay timeline of integrity events.
- **Desktop App Mode**: everything in Proctor Mode plus operating-system-level monitoring for a fully locked-down environment.

The centerpiece is the plagiarism engine. HackerRank reports its ML-based system hits [a 93% accuracy rate and is "three times more accurate" than traditional code-similarity methods](https://www.hackerrank.com/writing/how-hackerrank-catches-ai-generated-code-advanced-ml-plagiarism-detection). It works in two layers: a MOSS tokenizer that scores structural overlap between submissions, plus a machine-learning overlay that reads dozens of signals across coding behavior, submission patterns, and question features. That is a serious, well-built system for the job it does.

## What that stage is genuinely good at

For a take-home or timed coding assessment, HackerRank's approach is the right one. The task is one-directional: a candidate answers fixed questions inside a window you control, at a time of their choosing, with no interviewer present. In that setting, locking the environment and scoring the code for plagiarism makes complete sense. You can demand full-screen. You can disable the clipboard. You can replay the session afterward. Nothing about that is heavy-handed, because the whole point of the assessment stage is a controlled, comparable test.

If your funnel screens hundreds of applicants with an automated coding challenge, an assessment proctor is exactly what you want at that gate. This article is not arguing otherwise.

## Where the gap opens up

The gap appears the moment the funnel moves from "take this test" to "let's talk." A live interview breaks nearly every assumption the assessment stage depends on:

- **The window is gone.** In a live loop the candidate needs their camera, a screen share, maybe an IDE and a browser-based coding pad all at once. You cannot force full-screen or kill their tabs without breaking the interview itself.
- **You do not own the machine.** In an assessment you can push a desktop app and lock the OS. In a live interview you are a guest on someone's personal laptop for 45 minutes. Demanding a full lockdown is impractical and a bad candidate experience.
- **The threat moved off the test.** The tools candidates reach for in a live call are invisible AI overlays like Cluely and Interview Coder that render below the screen-share layer and feed answers in real time. They never touch a coding-test browser, so test-window proctoring is not aimed at them.
- **The pressure is real.** In a 2025 Blind survey of 3,617 professionals, [20% admitted secretly using AI during interviews and 55% called it the new norm](https://www.teamblind.com/blog/1-in-5-u-s-professionals-secretly-use-ai-during-job-interviews/). That pressure follows candidates straight into the live round, where the assessment proctor has no visibility.

None of this is a HackerRank flaw. It is a category boundary. Assessment proctoring secures the test window. It was never meant to police an open conversation.

## What Trueyy is built for

Trueyy starts at the live interview. It does not replace your Zoom, Meet, or Teams call, and it does not lock the candidate's machine. It sits beside the call and reads device-level signals on the candidate's computer in real time, with their consent, with no browser lockdown and no heavy install.

Instead of sealing a window, it watches for what a hidden AI assistant leaves behind. It scores risk roughly every 30 seconds across three surfaces: apps and screen activity, keystroke and paste patterns, and voice signals. It recognizes ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Cluely, Interview Coder, and 50-plus tools by the **structure of what they produce**, not a keyword blocklist, so a tool that renames itself doesn't slip through. Reading-gaze detection is on the roadmap, not live yet, so we don't count it as a current signal.

The privacy posture is deliberately lighter than assessment proctoring. No video is recorded or stored on Trueyy servers. Interview audio is transcribed, and screenshots are captured, encrypted, and retained per policy. There is no biometric identification, and a human always makes the final call. The output is a risk timeline to review, not a verdict the software hands down.

## Which one fits which stage

Plainly:

- **Use HackerRank** for the assessment gate: timed coding challenges, take-homes, and skills screens where the task is one person answering fixed questions in a window you control. Its plagiarism engine and lockdown modes are built for exactly that.
- **Use Trueyy** for the live interview: the two-way conversation on Zoom, Meet, or Teams, where the risk is real-time AI assistance and you can't (and shouldn't) lock down someone's personal machine.

The two are complementary, not competitive. Proctor the coding test with an assessment tool, then run the live interview with an interview tool. The failure mode is expecting one to cover the other's stage. ## A word on fairness and false positives

Whatever you deploy, the goal is a level field for honest candidates, not surveillance. Plagiarism flags and risk timelines are inputs to a human decision, never the decision itself. A nervous candidate, a non-native English speaker, or someone who pauses to think can trip a single signal without doing anything wrong. That is why Trueyy weighs multiple signals together, keeps a person in the loop, and asks for consent up front. Any monitoring you run should be transparent, consistent across candidates, and generous with the benefit of the doubt. A tool whose false positives you act on blindly is worse than no tool at all.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Trueyy a HackerRank proctoring alternative?

Not exactly, and that is the useful answer. HackerRank secures the coding-assessment stage; Trueyy secures the live interview stage. If you are replacing HackerRank's plagiarism-checked coding test, Trueyy is not that. If you are worried about hidden AI assistance during the live conversation that the assessment proctor never sees, Trueyy is the tool built for it. Most teams end up using both, at different points in the funnel.

### Does HackerRank proctoring catch ChatGPT during a coding test?

Within the test window, it is designed to. HackerRank's ML plagiarism model reads code structure, timing, and submission patterns to flag AI-generated work, and it reports [93% accuracy](https://www.hackerrank.com/writing/proctor-mode-vs-secure-mode-hackerrank-detects-chatgpt-ai-cheats-2025). That covers the submitted code on the assessment. It does not extend to a separate live interview happening later on a video call.

### Can HackerRank detect Cluely or Interview Coder in a live interview?

That is outside what test-window proctoring is built for. Overlays like Cluely and Interview Coder render below the screen-share layer during a live call and never touch a coding-test browser, so an assessment proctor is not aimed at them. Trueyy recognizes those tools and 50-plus others by the structure of their output and scores risk in near real time during the interview itself.

### What is the difference between coding assessment proctoring and live interview integrity?

Assessment proctoring controls a test environment: it locks the browser, disables copy and paste, and checks submitted code for plagiarism while a candidate answers fixed questions alone. Live interview integrity reads signals during an open, two-way conversation on a machine you do not own, where the candidate is supposed to talk and share their screen. One secures a window; the other listens for the tells of hidden assistance in a conversation.

### Do I need both HackerRank and Trueyy?

If your process has both a coding assessment and a live interview, yes, they cover different risks. HackerRank protects the take-home or timed test; Trueyy protects the live loop where real-time AI overlays show up. Using both means each stage is guarded by a tool actually built for it, instead of stretching one to cover a stage it was never designed for.
