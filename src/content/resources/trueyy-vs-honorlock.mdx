Honorlock is one of the most capable exam proctoring platforms on the market. That is precisely why it is the wrong tool for a job interview.

This is not a knock on Honorlock. It is a very good answer to a specific question — how do you secure a high-stakes online exam? — and a live remote interview is a different question with a different shape. Below is an honest comparison: what Honorlock does, why aiming it at an interview misfires, and which tool fits which job.

## What Honorlock is built for

Honorlock is remote **exam and certification** proctoring. Its model is a hybrid of AI monitoring and live human proctors: the AI flags suspicious behavior and a remote proctor can "pop in" to review or intervene. Around that sit a deep set of exam-integrity features, straight from its [AI detection page](https://honorlock.com/ai-detection-and-prevention/) and [product pages](https://honorlock.com/proctoring/):

- **BrowserGuard lockdown** — blocks websites, applications, and shortcuts like copy-paste, with an admin allowlist.
- **Webcam, screen, and audio recording**, plus ID verification and a room/environment scan before the test.
- **Secondary-device detection** — it flags phones and second screens, and reports that over 70% of violations involve mobile phones.
- **AI extension blocking and Smart Voice Detection** — it blocks unauthorized AI browser extensions and listens for voice-assistant wake words like "Hey Siri."
- **Search & Destroy** — it scans the web for leaked exam questions and issues takedowns.

It plugs into learning management systems — Canvas, Blackboard, Brightspace, Moodle — through LTI. For a certification exam or a proctored course test, that is a serious, coherent toolkit. Worth noting: Honorlock has **no interview product**. Its entire surface is exam integrity for education and certification.

## What breaks when you point exam proctoring at an interview

![A locked exam window with a padlock and a watchful eye beside a waveform threading between two silhouettes, contrasting a proctored test with live signal reading in a conversation.](/marketing/trueyy-vs-honorlock/proctor-vs-conversation.webp)

A live interview violates nearly every assumption Honorlock's model depends on.

- **There is no LMS and no owned container.** Honorlock launches an exam through an institution's LMS. A hiring interview has neither, so the whole delivery model has nowhere to attach.
- **You do not own the candidate's machine.** BrowserGuard plus a Chrome extension plus required software lock down the test-taker's environment. In hiring you are a guest on someone's personal laptop for 45 minutes; demanding that level of lockdown is impractical and a poor candidate experience.
- **An interview is not a closed-book test.** Honorlock's detections — copy-paste, AI extensions, phones, leaked questions — are exam-integrity events. The risk in a live interview is real-time AI assistance during an open, two-way conversation, which is a different signal entirely.
- **The surveillance level is calibrated for exams.** Room scans, continuous webcam and screen and audio recording, and long default retention make sense for institutional testing. They are heavy for a recruiter-candidate conversation on personal hardware.
- **The threat has moved off the browser.** The tools candidates actually reach for now are invisible AI overlays like Cluely and Interview Coder. They render below the screen-share layer and never touch the locked exam browser, so a lockdown approach is not aimed at them.

None of this is a Honorlock flaw. It is a category mismatch. You are reaching for a lock where you need a smoke detector.

The pressure behind that reach is real. In one 2025 survey of 3,617 professionals, [20% admitted secretly using AI during interviews and 55% called it the new norm](https://www.teamblind.com/blog/1-in-5-u-s-professionals-secretly-use-ai-during-job-interviews/). A separate Checkr survey of 3,000 managers found [59% suspect candidates misrepresent themselves with AI, while only 19% are confident their process would catch it](https://www.newsweek.com/most-managers-suspect-ai-fraud-in-hiring-survey-10477225). Exam lockdown does not close that gap for interviews, because the interview was never a locked window.

## Which tool wins which job

- **Choose Honorlock** for proctored exams, certification and licensure testing, and course assessments that live inside an LMS, where the organization controls the environment and lockdown is the point.
- **Choose Trueyy** for live, two-way interviews on Zoom, Meet, or Teams, where the risk is real-time AI assistance and you cannot — and should not — lock down someone's personal machine.

If your process has both a proctored test and a live interview, use the right tool for each. Proctor the exam with an exam tool; read signals in the interview with an interview tool. ## A word on fairness and false positives

Whatever you run, the goal is a level field for honest candidates, not surveillance. A nervous candidate, a non-native English speaker, or someone who pauses to think can trip a single signal without doing anything wrong. That is why Trueyy weighs multiple signals together, asks for consent up front, and keeps a person in the final decision. Any monitoring should be transparent, consistent across candidates, and generous with the benefit of the doubt.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can you use Honorlock for job interviews?

It is a poor fit. Honorlock is exam proctoring: it launches through an LMS, locks down the browser, and records the session while someone answers fixed questions. A live interview has no LMS, runs on a machine you do not own, and is a two-way conversation you cannot seal into a locked window. Honorlock also has no interview product; its entire focus is exams and certification.

### Does Honorlock work without an LMS?

Its model is built around delivery through a learning management system via LTI, plus a browser lockdown extension. That dependency is one of the main reasons it does not map onto a hiring interview, which has no LMS and no institution-owned test environment.

### Is Trueyy a Honorlock alternative?

For live interviews, yes; for exams, no. Trueyy is not exam-lockdown software and does not try to be. If you are running proctored tests, Honorlock or a similar exam proctor is the right category. If you are running conversational remote interviews and worried about hidden AI overlays, Trueyy fits the shape of that problem.

### Can Honorlock detect Cluely or Interview Coder overlays?

Honorlock focuses on locking down the exam browser and blocking AI browser extensions. Tools like Cluely and Interview Coder are desktop overlays that render below the screen-share layer and never touch the exam browser, so a lockdown-and-block approach is not aimed at them. Trueyy recognizes those tools and 50-plus others by the structure of their output during the live interview.

### How is Honorlock different from Proctorio?

Both are exam proctoring platforms, and both are exam tools rather than interview tools. Honorlock leans more on live human proctors who intervene during the test, plus phone detection and web scans for leaked questions; Proctorio leans on automated lockdown and suspicion scoring. For interviews, the distinction hardly matters — neither was built to read real-time AI assistance in a live conversation.
